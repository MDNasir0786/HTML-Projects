About the Forex Market
A 24×5 global marketplace where currencies are exchanged and opportunities appear around the clock.
What is Forex?
The foreign exchange (forex) market is the world’s largest financial market where currencies are bought and sold electronically through a global network of banks, institutions, and traders.
Why It Matters
Forex enables international trade, travel, and investment by allowing businesses and individuals to convert one currency into another and manage exchange‑rate risk.
Market Hours
Trading runs 24 hours a day, five days a week, as sessions in Sydney, Tokyo, London, and New York overlap and keep liquidity flowing across time zones.
Major Pairs
Most volume concentrates in major pairs such as EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and USD/JPY, which typically offer tighter spreads and deeper liquidity than exotic currencies.
Leverage & Risk
Leverage lets traders control large positions with small capital, but it magnifies both profits and losses, so disciplined risk management is essential.
Who Participates
Participants range from central banks and global institutions to hedge funds, corporations, and individual retail traders, all interacting in a decentralized over‑the‑counter market.
